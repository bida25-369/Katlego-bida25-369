Car Buying Tips


Before looking at specific models, take an inventory of your lifestyle to narrow down the body style and features that fit your routine.

Daily Purpose: Evaluate if the car is primarily for city commuting, hauling kids, or long highway trips.

Capacity Requirements: Consider family size and cargo needs. For example, a hatchback can offer flexibility for carrying large objects without the fuel costs of an SUV.

Usage Frequency: Factor in how often you drive and how long you plan to keep the vehicle, as this affects the value of features like fuel economy versus luxury.

Budget Beyond the Price Tag: Total cost of ownership includes insurance, fuel, and specialized maintenance (e.g., timing belt replacements)


Performance isn't just about speed; it's about how the car responds to your specific environment.
Acceleration and Braking: During a test drive, perform a "panic stop" to see if the car stays straight and check highway-speed acceleration to ensure it can merge safely.

Handling in Real Environments: Test the car in your own neighbourhood or on routes you typically drive, rather than a salesman's chosen "smooth path".

Fuel Economy vs. Power: Modern petrol and high-performance diesel engines now have negligible performance differences; choose based on your annual mileage (petrol is often better if you drive less than 16,000km/year).

Visibility and Ergonomics: Check blind spots and ensure the driving position is comfortable for your height without stooping or banging your head.
